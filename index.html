The Problem

Federal law bars certain repeat bankruptcy filers from receiving a Chapter 13 discharge. Section 1328(f) of the Bankruptcy Code creates mandatory waiting periods after a prior discharge. But no federal rule requires courts to verify eligibility before entering discharge.

The result: 391,951 prior-filer cases across 94 federal districts received discharge with zero eligibility verification. Some of those discharges were lawful. Some were not. Nobody checked.

Two sitting federal bankruptcy judges — Hon. Rebecca B. Connelly and Hon. Benjamin Kahn — have proposed amending the rules to require verification. Their proposal, Rules Suggestion 25-BK-N, is currently before the Advisory Committee on Bankruptcy Rules.

Rules Committee Submission

In March 2026, the Administrative Office of the U.S. Courts requested this project's empirical findings for posting as a related suggestion to Rules Suggestion 25-BK-N — a proposed amendment to Federal Rule of Bankruptcy Procedure 4004 that would require courts to verify discharge eligibility independently.

The data confirms that the gap identified by Judges Connelly and Kahn is systemic, not anecdotal. Converting Section 1328(f) enforcement from an objection model to a court-verified eligibility model would address a measurable compliance failure.
