Chapter 13 Report Card: Middle District of Louisiana

District Data FJC IDB FY 2008–2024 Court code: LAM

Summary

The Middle District of Louisiana had 11,260 Chapter 13 filings between fiscal years 2008 and 2024. Of these, 3,150 (28.0%) were prior filers. Among closed cases, 40.1% received a discharge and 59.9% were dismissed. 688 prior filers received a discharge — cases where §1328(f) eligibility screening should have occurred but cannot be verified from public data.

What This Means

Under 11 U.S.C. § 1328(f), a debtor who received a Chapter 13 discharge may not receive another Chapter 13 discharge if the second case was filed within two years of the first filing date. A debtor who received a Chapter 7 discharge may not receive a Chapter 13 discharge if the Chapter 13 case was filed within four years of the Chapter 7 filing date.

The FJC Integrated Database records whether a debtor had a prior bankruptcy filing (PRFILE field) but does not record whether the court verified §1328(f) eligibility before granting discharge. This means 688 discharges were granted to prior filers in this district without any publicly verifiable record of statutory eligibility screening.

Not every prior-filer discharge is necessarily improper. Many prior filers are outside the statutory time bars and fully eligible. But absent a verifiable screening mechanism, the number of potentially ineligible discharges in this district — and nationwide — is unknown.

Estimated Unscreened Cases

Of 688 prior-filer discharges in the Middle District of Louisiana, zero have a publicly verifiable §1328(f) eligibility determination in the FJC IDB. This is not unique to this district — no district records this data. The question is whether the screening happened and simply was not recorded, or whether it did not happen at all.
